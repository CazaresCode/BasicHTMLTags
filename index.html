Lesson One: Developing Your Arco Tone

3 Characteristics for a Solid Arco Tone

Objective: Identify the 3 characteristics for a solid arco tone and apply it to the double bass.

There are 3 primary characteristics that affect your bass tone:

  • Speed
  • Weight
  • Placement
Each characteristics depends on each other. This is a rough guide to help you get started.

Speed

The speed is the velocity of the bow. You can bow fast, slow, or something in between.
It is BEST to practice your slow speed first while gradually increasing the velocity after mastering a slow speed.

Weight

The weight (also known as pressure) should be natural arm weight to begin with.
You add more weight through your thumb (German) or index finger. (French)

Placement

The placement is the point of contact between the bow and string. There are two factors that determine placement:

  1. The location of the bow hair touching the string.
  2. The "lane" the bow is traveling on.

The placement of the bow can dramatically affect your timbre and tone.

Applying it to the Double Bass

All 3 characteristics work with each other. A good rule of thumb to remember is "Slow bow, low bow".

That addresses the speed and placement, but not the weight. The weight depends on your dynamic level and the bow placement on the string.

  • To get louder, add more weight
  • To get softer, reduce the weight
  • The further away you are from the frog, the more weight you add by rasing your arm to gain leverage.
